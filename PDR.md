# Project Name

* **CivicFlow**

# PRD Prompt 

Create a product requirements document for an interactive assistant that teaches users the election process in a simple, engaging, and step-by-step way. The product should explain election timelines, registration, candidate understanding, voting methods, ballot casting, counting, and result declaration. It should support beginners, first-time voters, and citizens who want a quick refresher. The experience must be easy to navigate, mobile-friendly, educational, and trustworthy.

The PRD should include product goals, target users, core features, user stories, content structure, UX principles, success metrics, non-goals, risks, and a phased roadmap.

# Product Requirements Document

## 1. Overview

CivicFlow is an interactive civic education assistant designed to help users understand how elections work from start to finish. The product simplifies complex election concepts into clear explanations, visual steps, timelines, and guided interactions.

The core purpose is to improve civic awareness and reduce confusion around voting procedures, election stages, and official processes.

## 2. Problem Statement

Many people, especially first-time voters, struggle to understand:

* how elections are conducted,
* what steps happen before voting,
* how to verify eligibility,
* where and when to vote,
* what happens after voting,
* and how election results are declared.

Current information is often scattered, formal, or difficult to understand. Users need a friendly assistant that makes the process clear, structured, and easy to follow.

## 3. Goals

* Educate users about the election process in simple language.
* Provide a step-by-step timeline of the election journey.
* Help users understand registration, eligibility, voting, and result stages.
* Offer an interactive learning experience for beginners.
* Build trust through clear, unbiased, and factual information.

## 4. Non-Goals

* Promoting any political party, candidate, or ideology.
* Allowing users to vote inside the app.
* Replacing official election commission websites.
* Providing legal or policy advice.

## 5. Target Users

### Primary Users

* First-time voters
* Students learning civic studies
* Citizens who need a refresher on election steps
* Users who want a quick and simple explanation of voting procedures

### Secondary Users

* Teachers and educators
* NGOs and civic awareness groups
* Parents helping younger voters understand elections

## 6. Core User Needs

* Understand the election process from beginning to end
* Learn what to do before election day
* Know how voting works
* Follow a timeline of major election milestones
* Ask questions in natural language
* Get concise answers or detailed explanations based on need

## 7. Product Scope

The product will include:

* Interactive election timeline
* Step-by-step process cards
* FAQ assistant/chat interface
* Visual guides and simple diagrams
* Country/region-neutral baseline content with localization support
* Glossary of key election terms
* Beginner mode and detailed mode

## 8. Key Features

### 8.1 Interactive Election Timeline

A guided timeline showing:

1. Voter registration
2. Candidate nomination
3. Campaign period
4. Election announcement
5. Polling day preparation
6. Voting process
7. Vote counting
8. Result declaration

### 8.2 Step-by-Step Learning Mode

Users can tap through each phase and see:

* What happens
* Why it matters
* What the voter should do
* Common mistakes to avoid

### 8.3 Ask Me Anything Assistant

Users can type questions like:

* “How do I register to vote?”
* “What happens on election day?”
* “How are votes counted?”
* “What documents may be needed?”

### 8.4 Simple Language Mode

The assistant should explain concepts in plain language with optional deeper detail.

### 8.5 Glossary

A built-in glossary for terms like:

* ballot
* constituency
* polling station
* voter ID
* nomination
* counting process

### 8.6 Reminder and Checklist Tools

Users can check off tasks such as:

* verify eligibility
* register before deadline
* check polling location
* prepare required documents
* review voting instructions

### 8.7 Visual Learning Support

Use icons, cards, progress bars, timelines, and short animations to make learning engaging.

## 9. User Stories

* As a first-time voter, I want a clear explanation of the election process so I know what to expect.
* As a student, I want a timeline of election stages so I can study quickly.
* As a user, I want to ask questions in my own words so I can get instant answers.
* As a teacher, I want a simple civic education tool to explain voting to learners.
* As a user, I want a checklist so I do not miss important steps before election day.

## 10. Functional Requirements

* Users can explore election stages in order.
* Users can search or ask questions using natural language.
* The assistant can return short answers or expanded explanations.
* The app should support a “beginner-friendly” mode.
* The app should show step indicators and progress.
* The app should include a glossary page.
* The app should allow localization for different regions or countries.
* The app should clearly mark content as informational and non-partisan.

## 11. Content Structure

### Main Sections

* Home / Welcome
* Election Timeline
* Step-by-Step Guide
* Ask Questions
* Glossary
* Checklist
* Learn More / Resources

### Suggested Learning Flow

1. What is an election?
2. Who can vote?
3. How do you register?
4. What happens before election day?
5. What happens on election day?
6. How are votes counted?
7. How are results announced?
8. What happens after results?

## 12. UX / UI Principles

* Simple and clean layout
* Large readable text
* Minimal jargon
* High contrast and accessible design
* Mobile-first interface
* Friendly illustrations and icons
* Clear call-to-action buttons
* Easy back-and-forth navigation

## 13. Accessibility Requirements

* Keyboard navigable interface
* Screen-reader friendly labels
* Color contrast compliant design
* Support for readable font sizes
* Short content blocks for easier scanning
* Optional audio narration for key steps

## 14. Trust and Safety

* Content must remain politically neutral.
* All educational information should be reviewed for accuracy.
* The system should avoid unsupported claims.
* The app must distinguish official procedures from general explanations.
* Sensitive civic information should include a note to verify with local election authorities.

## 15. Success Metrics

* Completion rate of the election learning flow
* Number of questions asked to the assistant
* Time spent on educational sections
* User satisfaction ratings
* Percentage of users who finish the checklist
* Return visits during election season

## 16. Analytics Events

* Opened election timeline
* Completed a step card
* Searched a glossary term
* Asked a question
* Completed checklist item
* Finished full learning flow

## 17. MVP Definition

The MVP should include:

* Welcome screen
* 6 to 8 core election stages
* Basic Q&A assistant
* Glossary
* Checklist
* Mobile-friendly UI
* Neutral educational content

## 18. Future Enhancements

* Multilingual support
* Regional election process templates
* Short animated explainers
* Audio narration
* Personalized learning paths
* Quiz mode for students
* Official resource linking by location

## 19. Risks and Mitigations

### Risk: Incorrect or outdated information

Mitigation: Use reviewed, region-specific content and update regularly.

### Risk: Political bias perception

Mitigation: Maintain neutral tone, balanced language, and clear boundaries.

### Risk: User confusion due to regional differences

Mitigation: Add localization and clearly label region-specific steps.

### Risk: Overly complex explanations

Mitigation: Provide beginner mode, simple language, and step-by-step breakdowns.

## 20. Roadmap

### Phase 1

* Build MVP with timeline, assistant, glossary, and checklist.

### Phase 2

* Add visual learning support, quiz mode, and better search.

### Phase 3

* Add localization, audio, animations, and official resource integration.

## 21. Summary

CivicFlow helps users understand elections in a simple, interactive, and trustworthy way. It turns a complicated civic process into an easy learning experience that is useful for first-time voters, students, and anyone who wants clarity about how elections work.
